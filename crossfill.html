introduction

what is crossfill?

crossfill is a b2b saas platform that gives brands visibility into how they appear in ai-generated search results — and tools to improve that presence. as llms like chatgpt, perplexity, and google's ai overviews increasingly answer questions directly, traditional seo metrics no longer tell the whole story.

i joined the team as an associate product designer, leading the internal admin experience — the set of tools that let our team manage client workflows, track optimization tasks, and ship recommendations at scale.

problem we had to solve

the optimization workflow lived outside the product

"teams were coordinating through slack, tracking in spreadsheets, storing drafts in google drive, and reviewing in docs. none of it connected. none of it scaled."

the core problem wasn't a ui problem — it was a process problem that needed a product solution. the optimization workflow was fragmented across multiple disconnected tools, creating unreliable tracking, duplicate work, human error, and slower publishing velocity.

⚡
manual handoffs between teams
every step required someone to manually notify the next person — no automation, no in-product state changes.
🔁
duplicated tracking systems
parallel records existed in both the product and spreadsheets — mismatched data, wasted effort, constant reconciliation.
📤
approvals outside the product
clients had to leave the dashboard entirely to review content in google docs before approving anything.
❓
unclear ownership of next steps
no clear task assignment in-product meant internal teams spent time following up manually rather than designing.
objective

design a dashboard that lets teams take action directly in the product

the goal was to move the full optimization cycle — from recommendation to approval to publishing — inside crossfill. no slack threads. no spreadsheets. no leaving the product to review a google doc.

"design a dashboard experience that supports the end-to-end optimization workflow and allows teams to take action directly in the product."

kickoff

how we started

before any sketching, i needed to understand what was actually happening — not what the intended workflow was, but what teams were doing in practice. i started by sitting in on customer success calls and mapping the real-world process step by step.

what became clear immediately was that the product as it stood handled data display well but had almost no support for action. it could tell you what to do — it couldn't help you do it.

discovery

what we found when we looked closely

discovery surfaced a pattern of repeated failure points at every transition between teams. the same problems kept appearing in different forms — notification failures, approval delays, context loss during handoffs.

the key insight was that these weren't process failures — they were product gaps. the product didn't support the actions teams needed to take, so teams built workarounds. the workarounds became the system.

three repeated failure points

01
no in-product notification

manual data entry everywhere
no automation between recommendation, approval, and publishing states. every status update required a human to manually update multiple systems.

customer success insights

what the people closest to clients were seeing

customer success was the team spending the most time bridging the gap between the product and client needs. their observations were essential — they had the clearest view of where clients got stuck, what questions kept coming up, and which manual steps were costing the most time.

insight 1 — clients needed reminders
clients consistently missed required actions because there were no in-product cues. cs was spending time on reminder messages that should have been automatic.
insight 2 — approval was the biggest bottleneck
the google docs review step was where work stalled most often. clients lost context between seeing a recommendation and reviewing the content draft.
insight 3 — internal teams played coordinator
cs was spending significant time simply advancing work through the pipeline — nudging approvals, updating statuses — instead of doing strategy work.
engineering capability review

scoping what was actually buildable

before committing to any design direction, i ran a feasibility review with engineering. i came in with a prioritized list of capabilities and we worked through what was possible within the timeline — and what needed to be deferred.

✅
confirmed — gsc data integration
blog recommendations could link directly to google search console data, giving clients real performance context alongside each action item.
✅
confirmed — in-dashboard accept/dismiss
clients could accept or dismiss recommendations directly in the product, triggering backend workflow state changes automatically.
✅
confirmed — permission-based admin controls
role-based access was technically feasible — a separate admin tab with login control could ship in v1.
🔜
deferred — inline content editor
an inline editor for reviewing and editing content directly in crossfill was technically possible but scoped to post-mvp. v0 would bridge the gap.
v0 — ideation

a deliberate bridge, not a shortcut

v0 was never meant to be the full solution. it was a deliberately lightweight stop-gap — a tracking and state management tool that gave teams a shared source of truth while the content editor was in development.

teams continued using google docs for content review during v0. but they now had a single place to track workflow state, reducing the dependency on slack and spreadsheets even before the full system was ready.

v0 ran for approximately one month. the constraint was intentional — building lightweight let us validate assumptions before committing to a larger solution.

"the goal was not to build the perfect product — it was to stop the bleeding and give teams a foundation to move faster."

admin controls

giving the internal team its own space

one of the first pieces scoped for v0 was a separate admin tab with login control. this gave the internal team visibility and management capability without surfacing admin functions to clients.

the admin controls were the foundation everything else was built on — without them, there was no clean separation between what clients saw and what internal teams needed to manage.

v1 — ideation

three features that closed the loop

v1 replaced the manual workflow with three interconnected features, each targeting a specific failure point. together they made the full optimization cycle operable inside the product for the first time.

content recommendations + calendar

recommendations with real context

the recommendations feature surfaced blog optimization tasks with potential impact scores, creation dates, and associated topics — linked directly to google search console data so clients had the performance context they needed to decide without leaving the product.

the calendar view let clients see their content pipeline over time, reducing the surprise of upcoming recommendations and giving them a sense of momentum and ownership over the process.

v1 recommendations view — accept/dismiss, impact scores, topic associations, gsc data integration
actions page layout
actions page — layout exploration
actions page — final shipped layout with clear ownership states

structure that shows who owns what

the actions page layout was designed to make ownership and status visible at a glance. based on a kanban structure, it organized tasks by state — not just by date — so teams could see what was waiting on a client, what was in progress, and what was ready to publish.

the original kanban exploration created ambiguity when blogs belonged to multiple topics simultaneously. the shipped version simplified to a list-first approach that preserved the state clarity without the structural confusion.

next steps

where crossfill goes from here

v1 closed the immediate gap. the roadmap is focused on pulling more of the optimization lifecycle fully in-product — eliminating the remaining external tool dependencies and building deeper integration with ai search performance data.

01 move review and drafting fully in-platform — eliminate remaining google docs dependency
02 inline editor for optimized content — write, review, and approve without leaving crossfill
03 deeper integration with search data and real-time ai performance insights
